Independent Kazakhstan
From 1991, December 16
And over 30 years

April 24, 1990

April 24, 1990 became an important historical date for Kazakhstan. On this day, the Supreme Council of the Kazakh SSR establishes the position of president. At the same time, the Supreme Council elected Nursultan Nazarbayev as president of the Kazakh SSR, who had headed the republic since June 1989.

October 25, 1990

Kazakhstan brought up the rear in the parade of sovereignties by adopting a declaration of state sovereignty on October 25, 1990. This document meant that from now on, in its policy, the state is guided exclusively by the current Constitution and its laws. In this case, we are still talking about the main document of the Kazakh SSR, which was adopted in 1978. However, despite the significance of this event, it was only the first step on the path to independence.

August 29, 1991

The first large-scale initiative of Kazakhstan was the closure of the Semipalatinsk test site. The corresponding decree was signed on August 29, 1991. This decision stopped a series of inhumane nuclear weapons tests in the Kazakh steppe and sent a message to the entire world community - Kazakhstan is a peaceful state.

December 1, 1991

That same fall, on October 16, the country adopted a law on the election of the President of the Kazakh SSR. The document determined the procedure and form of elections of the head of state. From that day on, only the people could choose the president, using their legal right to vote. So the first national presidential elections were held on December 1, 1991.

December 16, 1991

The first thing the elected head of state Nursultan Nazarbayev began to work on was the preparation of a law on state independence. The historical date of December 16, 1991 marked the appearance of the new state of the Republic of Kazakhstan on the world map. Kazakhstan was proclaimed as an independent, democratic, rule-of-law state. Literally a few days later, a law on citizenship was adopted, according to which everyone living in the country received the official status of citizens of the Republic of Kazakhstan. Türkiye, Russia, the USA and China were the first in the world to recognize the independence of Kazakhstan. Over the following weeks, 102 more countries joined them.
